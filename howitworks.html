How much can I afford to spend on a car while paying down my student loans?

We know how paralyzing student loans can feel. Unlike a home loan, student loans can also feel peskier because you don't have a visible asset like a home to support the payments.

We are here to tell you that you can take on additional debt if you need to purchase something like a car. However, understanding how much debt is critical to ensuring you live within your means and are still able to save for your future.

Your future isn't just about other goals like buying a home and traveling, it's also about your retirement. In order to understand how much car you can afford, we would consider your debt relative to your income. We can help you consider financing a car purchase vs. paying cash, and make sure you get a competitive auto loan rate. A Royal Monarch planner is here to make sure you get the right information and guidance.

How much can I spend on a house if I also want to have kids?

With so many moving pieces to your financial picture, a decision like this can be overwhelming. There’s no one way to answer this question because this decision cannot be made in a vacuum.

We look at the time horizon for buying a house and having children. We’ll need to consider future goals such as retirement and college funding as well. Once we have an idea of the overall picture, then we can adequately recommend a purchase price for your home that will not put your other goals at risk.

I’m 30 and I’m just starting to save ₹ 2,50,000 per year. When can I comfortably retire?

This is a great start to saving! To answer this question, we would want to consider your spending patterns and current account balances.

We would also want to understand future goals you might be saving for like a home, new car, or kids, as those affect your retirement fund. Healthcare is also important.

By taking a holistic view of your current financial situation and future financial goals we can provide a more precise answer to how early you could retire.
